Eating in Greensboro?

UNCG houses a variety of places to eat here on campus and if you are not satisfied with those options, there is countless more on Tate Street and beyond. This is your one-stop shop to find the most popular eats, as well as some of the most underrated. So get hungry, grab a couple of friends, and jump into UNCG's Eats!

Tate Street Coffee House

One of the staples of Tate Street, the Tate Street Coffee House has been serving UNCG students and Greensboro residents since 1993! The location serves both food and coffee as well as providing a relaxing work environment for everyone!

Menu Items

Black and Tan Latte

A fan favorite, the Black and Tan blends sweet and bitter perfectly to please any coffee drinker.

Sausage, Egg, and Cheese

Exactly what is sounds like with the added bonus of being able to choose your own bagel (Everything, Asiago Parmesan, etc.)!

Muffin

More of a sweets person? Tate St. Coffee has a good supply of pastries including muffins!

Hops Burger Bar

The best burger joint in Greensboro has two locations. Closest to UNCG is 2419 Spring Garden Street but there is also one on Battleground. You cannot go wrong with either one! It's open from 11am to 10pm everyday!

Menu Items

North Carolinian

My favorite burger from this location includes Applewood Smoked Bacon, Fried Green Tomato and an Egg. Can't get tired of it!

Parmesan and Truffle Fries

The perfect side to go with whatever burger you decide to grab! The parmesan and truffle mix perfectly.

Drafts

There are tons of drafts at Hops Burger Bar and you are sure to find something to drink!

Natty Greene's Downtown

In the heart of downtown you will find another bar joint that has a wide range of foods for everyone making it a perfect stop after your day in Greensboro. From Philly Cheesesteaks to wraps, you will community alongside eats at this location.

Menu Items

Elm Street Philly

A philly cheesesteak that definitely lives up to the cheese title! You're sure to be full after this sandwich and choice of side.

The Twister

Oversized Bavarian hot pretzel. Dark ale, mustard, and house cheese sauce.

Carolina Catfish

Cajun cornmeal breaded fresh catfish from Ayden, NC, served with remoulade.
